Phase 1
Customer Signup
From the moment a business discovers you to your first real conversation. Your goal is to respond fast — studies show contacting a lead within 5 minutes of form submission is 21x more effective.
01
Lead submits the landing page form
grandriverdigital-landing.html → auto-saves to your dashboard
Automated▼
When someone fills out the "Request your free mockup" form on your landing page, their info is automatically saved to localStorage and pushed into your dashboard as a new client record. You don't have to do anything manually.
What gets captured
First + last name and business name
Phone number (required) and email
Business type / industry
Which plan they're interested in (Starter / Growth / Pro)
Any notes they left about their business
Tools
Landing Page Form
Grand River Digital Dashboard
Your dashboard will show a green "New signup from landing page" banner the next time you open it. New clients appear at the top of your client list with a green dot.
02
You get notified — check your dashboard
Open grandriverdigital-dashboard.html → review new signup
Manual▼
Make it a habit to check your dashboard every morning. When you see a new signup, review their details before calling — know their business name, type, and which plan they selected.
Your checklist
Open grandriverdigital-dashboard.html and check for the green banner
Click into the new client record — read their name, business type, and notes
Look up their business on Google Maps to see their current online presence
Note their plan interest and prepare a rough talking point about it
Aim to call within 30 minutes of the form submission. The lead is hot — they just asked for help and they're thinking about their website right now.
03
First contact call (10 minutes)
Goal: qualify, build trust, book a follow-up
Manual▼
This call has one goal: make them feel heard and book a next step. Don't pitch. Don't sell. Just ask questions, show you've done your homework, and end with a clear next action.
Call script
Hi [Name], this is [Your Name] from Grand River Digital. I saw your request come in for [Business Name] — thanks for reaching out! I just took a quick look at your Google Maps listing and I can see you've got some great reviews. I'd love to help turn that into a full online presence. I have just a couple of quick questions so I can put together the right mockup for you: 1. What do most of your customers ask when they first find you? (Understand their needs) 2. Do you have any photos of your work or business I could use? 3. Is there a competitor in your area whose website you like the look of? Great — based on what you're describing, I think the [Starter/Growth/Pro] plan would be a great fit. I'm going to put together a free mockup this week. Can I send that over to your email on [day]? Perfect. I'll have something to show you by [date]. Talk soon!
Checklist
Call within 30 minutes of form submission if possible
Ask about their business, customers, and goals — don't just talk about your services
Commit to sending a mockup by a specific date
Log notes in HubSpot CRM immediately after the call
Tools
HubSpot CRM (free)
Your phone
Phase 2
Client Onboarding
Getting the paperwork and payment sorted before you touch a single pixel. Never start building without a signed contract and first payment received.
04
Send the proposal
HoneyBook → one link with pricing, scope, and contract
Manual · 15 min▼
In HoneyBook, create a proposal from your template. Customize the client name, business type, and which plan they discussed. The proposal includes your pricing, what's included, and a timeline — all in one professional document.
What to include in the proposal
Scope of work
Number of pages included
Services (hosting, maintenance, SEO)
What's NOT included (e.g., copywriting)
Revision rounds allowed (recommend: 2)
Pricing & timeline
Monthly plan price (Starter/Growth/Pro)
First month billed on signing
Site delivery timeline (48–72 hrs for mockup)
30-day notice cancellation policy
Tools
HoneyBook
Send the proposal within 24 hours of your call while you're still fresh in their mind. Follow up with a Loom video walking them through it — this dramatically increases conversion.
05
Contract signed & first payment received
HoneyBook handles e-signature + Stripe payment in one step
Automated▼
Your HoneyBook proposal includes a contract and payment link. The client signs digitally and pays their first month — all in one flow. You receive email confirmation. Do not start building until both are done.
Key contract clauses to always include
Payment is due on the 1st of each month, auto-charged
30-day written notice required to cancel
Client is responsible for providing content (text, photos) within 5 business days
You retain the right to pause work if payment is 7+ days overdue
Ownership of site transfers to client only if they move off your hosting
After signing
Confirm receipt via email: "Got it! Let's build your site."
Update client status in your dashboard to Active
Set up recurring billing in HoneyBook / Stripe
Never start work without payment. A signed contract with no payment means you're working on credit — that's how agencies lose money.
06
Send the client intake questionnaire
Google Form → collect everything you need to build
Manual · 5 min▼
Before you open Wix or Squarespace, you need content from the client. Create a simple Google Form and send it immediately after they sign. Give them a 3-day deadline to respond.
Intake form questions
What are your business hours?
What are the 3 main services/products you offer?
What sets you apart from competitors?
Who is your typical customer? (age, location, problem they have)
Do you have a logo? (upload) — if no, we'll create a simple one
Upload 5–10 photos of your business, work, or products
Any websites you like the look of? (paste links)
Preferred color scheme? (or "I trust you")
What do you want visitors to do on your site? (Call, book, order, visit)
Tools
Google Forms (free)
Google Drive (file storage)
Phase 3
Build the Website
From blank canvas to client-ready preview. Target: first mockup delivered within 48 hours of receiving intake responses.
07
Set up the client's tools
Domain, Wix project, Google tools — all before building
Manual · 30 min▼
Setup checklist — do this first, every time
Buy or transfer their domain (Namecheap or Google Domains ~$12/yr)
Create a new site in Wix Studio — name it [ClientName] for easy search
Set up Google Analytics 4 (free) — takes 10 min, tracks all future traffic
Set up Google Search Console (free) — connects site to Google's index
Check or create their Google Business Profile (GBP) — critical for local SEO
Add client to BrightLocal (if on SEO plan) — start rank tracking
Create a Google Drive folder with their name — store all assets here
Tools
Wix Studio
Google Analytics
Search Console
BrightLocal (SEO plan)
Namecheap (domains)
08
Build the website
Wix Studio → 48-hour turnaround target
Build · 4–8 hrs▼
Use the client's intake form answers, photos, and references to build. Start from a Wix template close to their industry, then customize colors, fonts, content, and layout.
Standard pages for every client (Starter plan)
Home — headline, key services, trust signals (reviews, years in business), CTA button
About — story, team photo if available, local connection
Services — list each service with a short description and photo
Contact — phone, email, address, embedded Google Map, contact form
Reviews / Testimonials — pull 3–5 from Google (always ask permission)
On-page SEO (do on every page)
Page title: "[Service] in [City], [State] | [Business Name]"
Meta description: 150 chars, includes main keyword and location
H1 tag on every page includes city name
Phone number clickable (tel: link) — critical for mobile
Images compressed and named descriptively (not IMG_1234.jpg)
09
Send mockup for client review
Loom video + preview link → client gives feedback
Manual · 20 min▼
Don't just send a link — record a 3-minute Loom video walking through the site. Narrate your design decisions. This prevents 80% of misunderstandings and makes you look incredibly professional.
Loom video script
Hi [Name], I'm excited to share the first look at [Business Name]'s new website! Let me walk you through what I've built... [Homepage] — I led with your strongest selling point: [X]. The call-to-action button goes to your contact page. [About] — I used the description you gave me and added your photos here. [Services] — I broke out your three main services. Let me know if you want any wording changed. [Contact] — Your phone number, address, and a form so people can message you directly. I'd love your feedback on: 1. Is the overall look and feel right for your brand? 2. Is anything missing that customers often ask about? 3. Any wording you'd like changed? Just reply to this email with your notes and I'll have revisions back to you within 24 hours. Excited to get this live for you!
Checklist
Record Loom walkthrough of the full site (3–5 min)
Send preview link + Loom link via email from your @grandriverdigital.agency address
Set a 3-day response deadline in HubSpot as a reminder task
Tools
Loom (screen recording)
Gmail (Google Workspace)
10
Handle revisions
2 rounds included — track requests, apply changes, resend
Manual · 1–2 hrs▼
Your contract includes 2 revision rounds. Apply all their feedback at once per round — don't do back-and-forth on individual items. After round 2, additional changes are billed at $75/hour.
Revision checklist
List every change requested in a Google Doc before touching anything
Apply all changes in one session
Record another short Loom showing what changed
If this is revision 2, note "this is your final round" in your email
Scope creep is real. "Can you also add a photo gallery, a booking system, and a blog?" after the design is approved = additional cost. Your contract should state this clearly, and so should you — politely but firmly.
Phase 4
Launch
Going from "approved by client" to live on the internet. This is the most exciting part — and the most error-prone. Work through the checklist methodically.
11
Pre-launch checklist
Test everything before connecting the domain
Critical · 45 min▼
Technical checks
Test on desktop, tablet, and mobile (use Chrome DevTools)
All links work — no 404s or broken buttons
Contact form submits and sends a confirmation email
Phone number is clickable (tap on mobile to call)
Google Maps embed loads correctly
All images load (no broken image icons)
Page speed test (Google PageSpeed Insights) — aim for 80+ on mobile
Content checks
Business name, address, and phone are correct on every page
No placeholder text (Lorem ipsum, "Your Name Here", etc.)
Spelling and grammar checked (paste into Grammarly)
Copyright year in footer is correct
Privacy policy page exists (required for contact forms / Google Analytics)
12
Go live
Connect domain → site is live on the internet
Launch day · 30 min▼
Launch steps
Connect domain in Wix (or point DNS records from Namecheap to Wix)
Enable SSL (Wix does this automatically — confirm the padlock shows in the browser)
Visit the live URL on your phone and computer to confirm it's working
Verify Google Analytics is receiving live data (check Realtime tab)
Submit sitemap to Google Search Console (speeds up Google indexing)
Update domain in your grandriverdigital-dashboard.html — change "pending.com" to live domain
Notify the client
Hi [Name]! Great news — [Business Name] is officially live at [domain.com]! I've just confirmed everything is working perfectly. Here's what's now in place: - Your website is live and secure (SSL protected) - Google Analytics is tracking your visitors - Google can now find your site Take a look and let me know what you think. Over the next 48–72 hours, Google will start indexing your pages and you should begin appearing in local searches. I'll be in touch next month with your first performance report. In the meantime, feel free to share your new website with your customers! — [Your Name]

Phase 5
Retain & Grow
This is where your agency becomes a real business. Monthly recurring work keeps clients paying and happy — and happy clients refer their friends. Your goal is zero churn.
14
Monthly maintenance (every client, every month)
~30 min per client · do on the 1st of each month
Recurring▼
This is what justifies the monthly fee. Block 2–3 hours on the first Monday of each month and go through every active client in your dashboard.
Monthly checklist (per client)
Check site is loading correctly (visit the live URL)
Wix auto-updates platform — confirm no display issues after updates
Check Google Analytics: any big drops in traffic? Any spike in errors?
Check contact form still submits (fill it out yourself)
Apply any content changes the client requested that month (via email)
Confirm SSL is still valid (browser padlock icon)
Confirm billing processed successfully in HoneyBook / Stripe
Growth plan clients: also update any seasonal content (holiday hours, promotions) and swap out one photo if they've sent new ones. This is what separates Growth from Starter.
15
Send monthly SEO report (Pro plan clients)
BrightLocal → auto-generates white-label PDF → email to client
Automated▼
BrightLocal automatically generates a branded PDF report showing Google Maps ranking, citation count, review changes, and local search visibility. Schedule it to send automatically on the 1st of each month.
Report email template
Hi [Name], Your [Month] website performance report is attached! Here are the highlights for [Business Name] this month: - Google Maps ranking for "[keyword]": #[X] (was #[Y] last month) - Website visitors: [N] people visited your site - Most popular page: [page name] - New Google reviews: [N] [If improving] You're making great progress! Keep encouraging happy customers to leave Google reviews — every one helps your ranking. [If stagnant] SEO takes time — typically 3–6 months to see major movement. You're building a strong foundation. Any questions about the report? Just reply to this email. See you next month! — [Your Name]

16
Upsell & upgrade conversations
Month 3+ · move Starter clients up, grow MRR
Recurring · Month 3+▼
At month 3, you have real data. Use it. If a Starter client is getting 200+ visitors/month, that's the perfect moment to show them what SEO could do — and upgrade them to Growth or Pro.
Upsell triggers to watch for
Starter client getting 150+ monthly visitors → pitch Growth (maintenance)
Growth client ranking #8–15 on Google → pitch Pro (SEO to push into top 3)
Client mentions competitors ranking above them → perfect SEO pitch moment
Client asks to add a feature outside their plan → upsell or bill hourly
Client refers you to someone → they're happy, ask for a Google review
Upsell script
Hi [Name], I was reviewing your site stats this month and noticed something exciting. You're getting [X] visitors a month and your phone number was clicked [Y] times. That means your site is working. Here's the thing — you're currently showing up at position [#] on Google for "[keyword]." With our Pro plan, I'd be targeting to push you into the top 3, which typically means 3–5x more calls. The difference is $[150/200] more per month. Based on what you're already seeing, I think one new customer a month from that higher ranking would more than cover it. Want me to put together a quick keyword report showing the opportunity?
The best time to upsell is right after delivering good news — a traffic increase, a new top-10 ranking, or a positive report. Lead with their win, then connect the upsell to getting more of it.
